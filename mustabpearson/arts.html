MUSIC & DANCE

The rhythm of our soul, the movement of our spirit

DANCE FORMS THAT DEFINE US

Every dance in Ghana tells a story - of celebration, of worship, of community, and of our shared identity as a people.

Kumasi

The royal court dances of the Ashanti Kingdom, performed with grace and dignity to honor tradition.

Adowa

A traditional Akan dance performed during funerals and social gatherings, expressing respect and unity.

Kpalongo

Energetic dance from the Ga people, celebrating life's joy and the spirit of togetherness.

Agbadza

Traditional Ewe dance originally from the Volta Region, now celebrated at events across Ghana.

Damba

Celebratory dance of the Dagomba people, honoring the birth of the Prophet Mohammed.

Fante

Coastal dance from the Fante people, reflecting the maritime heritage of Ghana's coastline.

THE HEARTBEAT OF PATRIOTISM

The drum is the soul of Ghanaian music. In every rhythm, we hear the voice of our ancestors, the pulse of our communities, and the heartbeat of our nation.

When we beat the drums, we celebrate our heritage. When we dance to traditional rhythms, we honor those who came before us and inspire those who will come after.

TheTalking Drum, or "Gangan," mimics the human voice and has been used for centuries to communicate stories, news, and values across Ghanaian communities. It is a symbol of our connected heritage.

PRESERVING TRADITION IN MODERN TIMES

Today's Ghanaian musicians blend traditional rhythms with contemporary sounds, keeping our heritage alive while reaching new audiences.

Highlife

Born in the early 20th century, Highlife combines traditional Ghanaian rhythms with Western instruments. It remains a symbol of Ghanaian musical innovation and pride, celebrated by legends like E.T. Mensah and modern artists who keep the genre alive.

Hiplife

A fusion of highlife and hip-hop that emerged in the 1990s. Artists like Reggie Rockstone and Sarkodie use this genre to tell Ghanaian stories, celebrate our culture, and inspire the youth to embrace their identity.

Afrobeat

Ghana has contributed immensely to the Afrobeat movement, with Fela Kuti being a pioneer. Today's Ghanaian Afrobeat artists continue this legacy, creating music that resonates across Africa and the diaspora.

Patriotic Music

From independence anthems to modern songs celebrating Ghana, patriotic music unites us. Songs like "Ghana" by Pat Thomas and contemporary hits remind us of our shared values and national pride.
